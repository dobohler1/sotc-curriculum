3 Step 2: Forward AND Backward

Add two more blocks below your existing program:

when program starts
set movement motors to A + B
move forward for 2 seconds
wait 1 seconds ← CONTROL menu (orange)
move backward for 2 seconds
How to get "backward": Drag a new move forward block. Click the word forward — it's a dropdown. Change it to backward.

Click Play.

Did the robot return to its starting position?

If it didn't land exactly on the start, why do you think that is?

4 Step 3: Add the Backup Beep

Insert one new block from the SOUND menu (purple), right before the backward move:

when program starts
set movement motors to A + B
move forward for 2 seconds
wait 1 seconds
play beep 72 for 0.5 seconds ← NEW! SOUND menu
move backward for 2 seconds
What's the number 72? That's the pitch (like a musical note). Higher number = higher pitch. Try changing it!

Click Play. Did it beep before reversing?  Yes / No

Experiment: Try changing the beep number. What did you try, and what happened?

To draw a face: Click the pattern square on the turn on block. A 5x5 grid pops up. Click pixels to light them up. Draw a happy face for forward and a sad face for backward!

6 The Race: 10-Foot Round Trip

Your robot must:

  1. Drive forward exactly 10 feet (to the tape line)
  2. Stop
  3. Beep
  4. Reverse back to the exact starting position

How to tune it

You have two numbers to adjust:

What to Change Where Try These Values
Duration (how long it moves) The number in your move forward block 4, 6, 8, 10 seconds
Speed (how fast it moves) Add a set movement speed block at the top 30%, 50%, 75%
Pro tip: Slow + long is easier to control than fast + short. And make sure your forward and backward durations match!
