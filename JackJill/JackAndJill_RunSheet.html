Instructor Run Sheet

Build It. Code It. Race It. — Jack & Jill Cluster — 1 Hour

Before the Event (Day Before)

  • Pre-build all driving bases (wheels, 2 motors in A+B, hub charged)
  • Attach distance sensor to Port D on one demo robot
  • Load JackAndJill_SensorDemo.py onto the demo robot and test it
  • Test every robot: turn on hub, verify Bluetooth connection to a laptop
  • Open SPIKE app on every laptop, confirm it launches
  • Open the Google Doc worksheet on every laptop (or bookmark it)
  • Print flyers (one per family)
  • Lay 10-foot tape line on the floor with start mark
  • Lay a second tape line at 12 inches and 6 inches from start (for scoring return)
  • Set up 2-3 obstacles (boxes, books) for the sensor demo area
  • Have a timer ready on your phone
  • Slides loaded: open JackAndJill_Showcase_Slides.html on projector laptop

Minute-by-Minute

Time Slides What You Do What Kids Do
0:00–0:03 Slide 1 Demo the sensor robot live. No slides yet — just hold it up, set it on the ground near some obstacles, and let it go. Let the room react.
Then show Slide 1: "That's what we're building toward. But first — let's start with the basics." 		Watch. Get hyped.

Common issue: Kids add a second "set movement motors" block. They don't need it — only need it once at the top. 		Add 2 blocks. Play. Robot goes forward, pauses, comes back. Worksheet answers.
0:22–0:27 Slide 9 Step 3: Backup Beep. Show the Sound menu. Let them experiment with different beep numbers for a minute.
Let this moment breathe. Kids love making weird sounds. The room gets loud and fun — that's the point. Moms are watching.
 Add beep block. Experiment with pitch numbers. Fill in beep table on worksheet.
0:27–0:30 Slide 10 Step 4: Light Matrix. Show them the Light menu. Click the pattern grid and draw a face live. "You've got 2 minutes to make your own face."
Fast finishers can try Slide 10 face swap (happy forward, sad backward). 		Draw face patterns. Play. Sketch designs on worksheet grid.
0:30–0:32 Slides 11–12 SWITCH roles. New driver starts fresh project, builds from memory. Navigator coaches.
While they rebuild: show Slide 12 (Python Reveal). Point out the side-by-side. "Same program, different language. This is what professional engineers write."
This is the mom moment. Walk to where parents are and say: "What you're seeing right now is your child's first exposure to the same language taught in college CS programs."
 Switch. Rebuild. Look at Python comparison.
0:32–0:35 Slides 13–14 Announce THE RACE. Explain the rules. Show the scoring. Point to the tape line on the floor.
"You have 8 minutes to tune your robot. Then 3 official runs." 		Read race rules on worksheet.
0:35–0:43 Slide 14 Tuning time. Walk the room. Help teams adjust duration and speed. Encourage them to use the test log on the worksheet.
If a team is stuck: Suggest speed=40, duration=6 as a starting point. Let them iterate from there. 		Test, adjust, test, adjust. Fill in test log.
0:43–0:52 Slide 15 Official race runs. One team at a time on the track. Everyone else watches.
Call out scores live. Ham it up. "Ohhh, 8 inches! So close!"
Have moms line up along the track to watch.
3 runs per team, best score counts. Keep pace moving — ~1 min per run. 		Race! Fill in scoring table. Cheer for other teams.
0:52–0:55 Slide 16 Sensor demo. "You just made a robot that follows YOUR instructions. But what if it could think for itself?"
Place the demo robot on the ground near obstacles. Run the sensor program. Let it navigate.
"That's Session 4 of the full course." 		Watch. Gasp.
0:55–1:00 Slides 17–18 Walk through the 6-session course. End on Slide 18: "You Did That."
"If your kid wants to come back — grab a flyer on your way out."
Hand out flyers to every parent.
Have kids finish the reflection on the worksheet if time. 		Reflection. Talk to parents.

Emergency Fixes

ProblemFix
Robot won't connect Turn hub off and on. Re-pair via Bluetooth in SPIKE app. Make sure no other laptop is connected to that hub.
Robot drives in a curve Motors might be in wrong ports. Check: left motor cable goes to Port A, right to Port B. Or swap A+B in the set movement motors dropdown.
Robot drives backward when it should go forward Motors are reversed. Swap the dropdown from A+B to B+A.
No sound on beep Volume might be 0. Add a set volume to 100% block from Sound menu at the top of the program.
"Word Blocks" option not visible Make sure they're on SPIKE 3 (not the old SPIKE Legacy app). Check for app updates.
Kid finishes way ahead of others Challenge them: "Can you make it play a sound WHILE it drives? Can you make it do a spin at the end?"
Kid is disengaged / lost Switch them to Navigator role. Give them the worksheet and have them read steps to their partner. Everyone contributes.
