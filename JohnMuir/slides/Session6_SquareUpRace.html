Race Rules

Counts as a clean run

Robot stays inside the 1-foot lane for all 4 sides. Beeps before every turn. Blinker shows before every turn. Completes the full square.

Eliminated (that attempt)

Any wheel crosses outside the tape. Missing a beep or blinker on any turn. Robot stops mid-course. Robot doesn't complete all 4 sides.

  • Unlimited attempts. Failed run? Go back, adjust your code, try again.
  • Fastest clean time wins. Dorian times each run with a stopwatch.
  • You can change your code between attempts. That's the point — iterate.

Tuning Time (10 min)

Open your code from last session. You have 10 minutes to test and tune before official runs start.

Think about this: faster velocity = shorter time.

But faster also means less control. Find the speed where your robot is fast AND stays in the lane.

  • Test on the open floor first — is your square still square?
  • Then do a practice run on the course — does it stay in the lane?
  • Adjust. Test. Adjust. Test.

How the Race Works

  1. Dorian calls your name
  2. Place your robot at START, facing the first straight
  3. Press play. Hands off. Step back.
  4. We all watch. Dorian starts the timer.
  5. If your robot leaves the lane — that attempt is done. Go tune and try again.
  6. If your robot completes the square — Dorian records your time.
  7. Want to beat your own time? Get back in line.
