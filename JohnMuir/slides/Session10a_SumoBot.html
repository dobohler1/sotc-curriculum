Torque vs Speed

Gear ratios: the fundamental trade-off

More Pushing Power

Small gear → Large gear (o) →→→ ( O ) Motor spins fast, wheel spins slow = MORE TORQUE

Slower but much harder to push.

More Speed

Large gear → Small gear ( O ) →→→ (o) Motor spins slow, wheel spins fast = MORE SPEED

For sumo: what do you want?

It depends on your strategy.

Three Sumo Strategies

►►

Bull Rush

Detect the opponent and charge at full speed. Pure aggression. Hit hard, push fast.

Needs: speed + strong front

↻

Searcher

Spin in place to scan for the opponent. When detected, charge. Smart and methodical.

Needs: good sensor + fast reaction

◎

Edge Dancer

Stay near center. Use edge detection to avoid falling out. Let the opponent come to you.

Needs: color sensor + patience

Each table: which strategy will you try?

You can combine them. You can invent your own.

Design Your SumoBot

Start with your existing driving base. Modify it for sumo.

Front Pusher

Add a wedge or flat plate to the front. Scoop under your opponent.

Lower the Hub

Move the hub closer to the ground. Low center of mass = harder to push.

Widen the Wheelbase

Spread your wheels apart for better stability. Don't get tipped.

Sensor Placement

Distance sensor faces forward. It needs a clear line of sight to the opponent.

The Key Code Pattern

This is the core of every sumo bot

while True:
    dist = distance_sensor.distance(3)  # Port D

    if dist != -1 and dist < DETECT_DISTANCE:
        # CHARGE!
        motor_pair.move(motor_pair.PAIR_1, 0,
            velocity=ATTACK_SPEED)

    else:
        # SEARCH — spin to find opponent
        motor_pair.move(motor_pair.PAIR_1, 100,
            velocity=SEARCH_SPEED)

    await runloop.sleep_ms(50)

DETECT_DISTANCE = how close before you charge  •  ATTACK_SPEED = charge speed  •  SEARCH_SPEED = spin speed
