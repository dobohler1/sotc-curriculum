The single most important thing this session: Circulate Red Table for the first 15 minutes. These students have never gotten code running. They don't need a lecture — they need you standing behind them catching typos as they type.

Minute-by-Minute

TimeWhat HappensYour Role
0–5 Show slides 1–2. Call out names by table. Students move. Direct traffic. No negotiating on table assignments.

Common Issues You'll Hit

ProblemFix
"My robot won't connect" USB is most reliable (green ring on hub). If Bluetooth, only one laptop at a time per hub. Try turning hub off and on.
"I typed it exactly but it says error" Check: underscore vs space (light_matrix not light matrix), colon after main():, indentation (4 spaces for everything inside main), await not awalt.
"My motors spin but the robot doesn't drive" Motors are in wrong ports or paired backwards. Try swapping the two numbers in motor_pair.pair().
"I already know how to do this" Great — move to Green Table. Grab the Showcase Routine from Google Classroom.
Student sitting on phone Laptop open, phone in bag. If they won't start, sit next to them and type the first line together. Getting the first line on screen is the hardest part.

3. Session 2: Prove It — Coach G's Playbook

The single most important thing this session: Every student demos. No exceptions, no skipping, no "I'll do it later." Even if their code doesn't run, they come to the table and explain where they're stuck. A score of 1 is infinitely better than a score of 0.

Before Students Arrive

  • Open Session 2 - Prove It Slides.html in Chrome/Safari, go full screen
  • Set up one "demo table" at the front with a robot ready to go
  • Have your roster ready to check off scores (printed or on your phone)

Minute-by-Minute

TimeWhat HappensYour Role
0–3 Slide 1 (title). Quick overview: today you demo. Set expectations. "Everyone presents today. No exceptions."
3–8 Slide 2 (Bug Hunt). Students find 3 bugs silently. Hands up, don't shout. First 3 correct answers demo first. This creates a competitive incentive.
8–10 Slide 3 (answers). Quick review. Keep it fast. "Underscore, colon, spelling. That's it. These are the same mistakes we keep making."
10–20 Slide 4. Open work time. Last chance to get code ready. Circulate. Priority: anyone who still doesn't have working code from Session 1.
20–40 Slides 5–7. Demo circuit begins. Call students one at a time. Score live. Keep it moving — 90 seconds each. Students waiting continue working.
40–45 Slide 8–9. Submit final code + written reflection. Anyone who updated code resubmits. Everyone answers the written question.

4. Green Table: Showcase Routine Challenge

Assignment 6 is already in Google Classroom with the full brief — requirements, scoring rubric, example code skeleton, and submission instructions. You do not need to explain it to them.

What to say: "You're at Green Table. Open Google Classroom, find Assignment 6: Showcase Routine Challenge. Read the requirements, grab a robot, and work independently. You'll demo your routine in Session 2."

That's it. These students are self-directed. Let them read the assignment themselves, figure out the code, and test on their own. If they have a question, they should try to solve it before asking you — that's part of the challenge.

A Note on Vincent and Winston

These two don't need hand-holding. Give them the assignment and get out of their way. If they finish early, the best use of their time is helping a Red or Yellow Table student get their code running — but they should point at the screen and explain, not type for the other student. If they take over someone's keyboard, the other student learns nothing.

If Jacob Peterson returns, do not automatically put him at Green Table. Have him work at Yellow Table first and demonstrate independently that he can write and explain code without Vincent. If he can, he earns Green Table. If he can't, that's important information.
