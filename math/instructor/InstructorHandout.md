# SOTC MATH COMPETITION - INSTRUCTOR HANDOUT

---

## THE GAME IN 30 SECONDS

1. Show problem → kids solve at their tier
2. Timer ends → pencils down
3. Show answers → captains check
4. Bonus points for good questions or finding mistakes
5. Captain reports Yes/No for each tier → record score → next round

---

## TIER LEVELS

| Tier | Who Does It |
|------|-------------|
| **TIER A** | Grades 1-3 |
| **TIER B** | Grades 4-7 (including captains) |
| **BONUS** | Anyone who finishes early |

### THE LEVEL-UP RULE

| | Allowed? |
|---|----------|
| Younger kid does harder tier | YES - "Leveling up!" |
| Older kid does easier tier | NO - Doesn't count |

*Example: 5th grader does Tier A = NO points. 2nd grader does Tier B = YES points.*

---

## SCORING

| What | Points |
|------|--------|
| Team got Tier A correct (by a Gr 1-3 student) | **2 pts** |
| Team got Tier B correct (by a Gr 4-7 student) | **2 pts** |
| Team got Bonus correct (anyone) | **2 pts** |
| Good question asked | **+1 pt** |
| Found mistake in answer | **+3 pts** |

**MAX BASE SCORE PER ROUND = 6 pts**

---

## ROUND TIMING (5 min total)

| Time | Action | What to Say |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 0:00 | Show problem | *"Eyes on screen. Tier A is... Tier B is..."* |
| 0:30 | Start timer | *"3 minutes. GO!"* |
| 3:00 | Warning | *"30 seconds!"* |
| 3:30 | Stop work | *"PENCILS DOWN!"* |
| 3:30 | Show answers | *"Here are the answers. Captains, check your team."* |
| 4:15 | Bonus points | *"Any questions? Anyone find a mistake?"* |
| 4:30 | Collect scores | *"Captains, report. Tier A yes or no?"* |
| 5:00 | Next round | Update scoreboard, move on |

---

## COLLECTING SCORES (Say this each round)

> *"Red Team—Tier A, yes or no? Tier B? Bonus?"*
>
> *"Blue Team—Tier A, yes or no? Tier B? Bonus?"*
>
> *(repeat for Gold, Green)*

Circle Y or N on score sheet. Add points. Done.

---

## LATE ARRIVALS

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Ask: *"Name? Grade?"* |
| 2 | Assign to smallest team |
| 3 | *"Grab paper, sit with [team]. Do Tier [A/B] based on your grade."* |

Takes 10 seconds. They're in.

---

## TROUBLESHOOTING

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| No captain on team | Oldest student reports |
| Older kid claims Tier A | *"Doesn't count—do Tier B"* |
| Team has no young kids | They skip Tier A points that round |
| Team has no older kids | Young kids can level up to Tier B |
| Kids talking during work | *"Silent work time!"* |
| Argument about answer | You have final say. Move on. |

---

## OPENING SCRIPT (Read at start of session)

> *"Welcome to the Math Competition!*
>
> *I show a problem. Solve it at YOUR level—Tier A for grades 1-3, Tier B for grades 4 and up.*
>
> *Important: You can level UP but not DOWN. If you're in 4th grade or higher, Tier A doesn't count for you.*
>
> *When timer ends, pencils down. I show answers. Captains check your team and report yes or no for each tier.*
>
> *Bonus points if you ask a good question or find a mistake in my answer.*
>
> *Team with the most points wins. Let's go!"*

---

## CLOSING SCRIPT

> *"Let's add up the scores!*
>
> *Red Team total... Blue Team... Gold... Green...*
>
> *The winner is [TEAM]! High-fives all around!"*
