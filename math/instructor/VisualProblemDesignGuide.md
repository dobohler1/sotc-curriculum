📘 SOTC VISUAL PROBLEM DESIGN GUIDE

Designing Math & STEM Problems for Students Who Struggle With Reading

⸻

🎯 Core Principle

Students should be able to understand the task by looking, not reading.
Text supports visuals. Visuals drive understanding.

⸻

🧠 Cognitive Load Rules
	•	One idea per problem
	•	One diagram per problem
	•	Fewer than 12 words when possible
	•	Numbers bigger than words
	•	Same layout every time

⸻

🧩 Diagram Design Rules

Use Only Simple Shapes

Arrows, boxes, circles, straight lines

Show Action With Arrows

→ Forward
← Backward
↺ Turn Left
↻ Turn Right

Label With Numbers + Units Only

✔ 50 cm
✔ 4 s
✘ “moves forward fifty centimeters”

One Quantity Per Label

✔ 50 cm
✔ 4 s
✘ 50 cm in 4 seconds

Place Labels Next to What They Describe

Distance → on arrow
Time → near clock icon
Angle → near turn

⸻

🔣 Icon Anchors (Use Everywhere)

📏 Distance
⏱ Time
⚡ Speed
📐 Angle
❓ Question

Icons replace reading.

⸻

🎨 Color Coding (Optional but Powerful)

Blue → Distance
Green → Time
Orange → Speed
Purple → Angle

If no color, rely on consistent placement.

⸻

🔢 Number-First Format

80 cm
4 s
Speed = ?

Not:
“What is the speed if…”

⸻

📐 Layout Template (Use for All Problems)

[ DIAGRAM ]

Distance: ___
Time: ___
Answer: ___

or

Distance = ___
Time = ___
Speed = ___

⸻

📊 Table Option

Trial	📏	⏱	⚡
1	40	2	___
2	80	4	___


⸻

🔁 Pattern Repetition

Keep structure identical across problems.
Change only numbers.

⸻

🧱 Scaffolding Order

Fill-in blanks → Multiple choice → Open answer

⸻

👂 Multi-Modal Teaching

Teacher reads aloud
Students point to numbers
Students circle arrows

⸻

🔤 Font & Spacing

Sans-serif font
18 pt minimum
Lots of white space

⸻

🚫 Avoid

Paragraphs
Background images
Long instructions
Decorative art

⸻

✅ Final Quality Check

☐ Can I solve it without reading?
☐ One idea only?
☐ Labels next to objects?
☐ Numbers bigger than words?

If yes → ready to use.
