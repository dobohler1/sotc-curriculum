Quarter 1: "Warm-Up Drills"

Tier 1 How many basketballs?

ANSWER: 10 basketballs
What students see:
A pyramid of basketballs (4 on bottom row, 3, 2, 1 on top)
Solution:
Count each row: 4 + 3 + 2 + 1 = 10
If student is stuck:
"Start at the bottom and count each row. How many on the bottom? Now the next row up?"
Common mistakes:
Miscounting — have them point to each ball as they count

Tier 2 How many bags?

ANSWER: 8 bags
What students see:
48 basketballs, 6 per bag
Solution:
48 ÷ 6 = 8 bags
If student is stuck:
"If each bag holds 6 balls, how many groups of 6 are in 48? Count by 6s."
Common mistakes:
Multiplying instead of dividing (gets 288)

Tier 3 How many MORE old than new?

ANSWER: 10 more old balls (accept 9 or 10)
What students see:
48 basketballs. 40% are new. Rest (60%) are old.
Solution:
  1. New balls: 48 × 0.40 = 19.2 ≈ 19
  2. Old balls: 48 × 0.60 = 28.8 ≈ 29
  3. Difference: 29 − 19 = 10 more old
Shortcut: 60% − 40% = 20% → 48 × 0.20 = 9.6 ≈ 10
If student is stuck:
"What's 10% of 48? (4.8) So what's 40%? (4.8 × 4 = 19.2)"
Common mistakes:
Just subtracting percentages (60 − 40 = 20) — that's not the answer!

Varsity How many players?

ANSWER: 27 players
What students see:
Team has 20–35 players
• Rows of 3: 0 left over (divides evenly)
• Rows of 4: 3 left over
• Rows of 5: 2 left over
Solution:
  1. List multiples of 3 in range: 21, 24, 27, 30, 33
  2. Check "remainder 3 when ÷ 4":
    • 21 ÷ 4 = 5 R1 ✗
    • 24 ÷ 4 = 6 R0 ✗
    • 27 ÷ 4 = 6 R3 ✓
    • 30 ÷ 4 = 7 R2 ✗
  3. Verify: 27 ÷ 5 = 5 R2 ✓
If student is stuck:
"Start with numbers that divide evenly by 3. Then check each one against the other two rules."
Common mistakes:
Stopping after checking only one condition (picks 21 or 24)

Quarter 2: "The Practice Court"

Tier 1 Total feet?

ANSWER: 100 feet
What students see:
Player runs baseline (50 ft) 2 times
Solution:
50 + 50 = 100 ft (or 50 × 2 = 100)
If student is stuck:
"If you run 50 feet, then run 50 feet again, how far total?"

Tier 2 Perimeter?

ANSWER: 288 feet
What students see:
Court is 94 ft long × 50 ft wide
Solution:
Perimeter = all 4 sides = 94 + 94 + 50 + 50 = 288 ft
Or: P = 2(94) + 2(50) = 188 + 100 = 288
If student is stuck:
"Perimeter means walking ALL the way around. A rectangle has 4 sides — two long ones and two short ones."
Common mistakes:
Calculating AREA instead (94 × 50 = 4,700) or only adding two sides (144)

Tier 3 Speed in ft/sec?

ANSWER: 6.4 ft/sec
What students see:
Player runs 288 ft in 45 seconds
Solution:
Speed = Distance ÷ Time = 288 ÷ 45 = 6.4 ft/sec
If student is stuck:
"Speed is how far you go in ONE second. If she went 288 feet in 45 seconds, divide to find one second."
Common mistakes:
Dividing backwards (45 ÷ 288 = 0.156)

Varsity When does B catch A?

ANSWER: 15 seconds
What students see:
Player A: 6 ft/sec | Player B: 8 ft/sec | B starts 30 ft behind A
Solution (Relative Speed):
  1. B gains on A at: 8 − 6 = 2 ft/sec
  2. Gap to close: 30 ft
  3. Time = 30 ÷ 2 = 15 seconds
Solution (Algebra):
Position A = 6t, Position B = 8t − 30
Set equal: 6t = 8t − 30 → 30 = 2t → t = 15
If student is stuck:
"B is faster by how much per second? (2 ft/sec) How long to gain 30 feet at that rate?"
Common mistakes:
Dividing 30 by just 8 or 6 (gets 3.75 or 5), forgetting the head start

Quarter 3: "Game Stats"

Tier 1 How many MORE pts do Lions have?

ANSWER: 6 points
What students see:
Scoreboard: Lions 24, Tigers 18
Solution:
24 − 18 = 6 points
Common mistakes:
Adding instead (24 + 18 = 42)

Tier 2 Total points?

ANSWER: 20 points
What students see:
Marcus made: 4 free throws (1pt), 5 two-ptrs (2pt), 2 three-ptrs (3pt)
Solution:
  • Free throws: 4 × 1 = 4
  • Two-pointers: 5 × 2 = 10
  • Three-pointers: 2 × 3 = 6
  • Total: 4 + 10 + 6 = 20 points
Common mistakes:
Adding just the counts (4 + 5 + 2 = 11) instead of multiplying first

Tier 3 Shooting %?

ANSWER: 60%
What students see:
Marcus took 20 shots, made 12
Solution:
Shooting % = (Made ÷ Taken) × 100 = (12 ÷ 20) × 100 = 60%
Or: 12/20 = 6/10 = 60%
Common mistakes:
Dividing backwards (20 ÷ 12 = 167%)

Varsity Shots taken today?

ANSWER: 12 shots
What students see:
BEFORE: 40% shooting, 60 shots taken
TODAY: Made ALL shots
NOW: 50% all-time
Solution:
  1. Before: 40% of 60 = 24 makes out of 60 shots
  2. Let x = shots today (all made)
  3. New equation: (24 + x)/(60 + x) = 0.50
  4. Solve: 24 + x = 0.5(60 + x)
    24 + x = 30 + 0.5x
    0.5x = 6
    x = 12 shots
Check: 36/72 = 50% ✓
If student is stuck:
"She made 24 out of 60. Both the numerator AND denominator increase by x. What value makes it equal 50%?"
Common mistakes:
Setting up (24 + x)/60 = 0.5 — forgetting denominator also increases!

Quarter 4: "Championship Round"

Tier 1 Total medals?

ANSWER: 10 medals
What students see:
5 players, each gets 2 medals
Solution:
5 × 2 = 10 medals

Tier 2 Total GAMES played?

ANSWER: 7 games
What students see:
Tournament bracket: 8 → 4 → 2 → 1 (champ)
Solution:
  • Round 1: 8→4 teams = 4 games
  • Round 2: 4→2 teams = 2 games
  • Finals: 2→1 = 1 game
  • Total: 4 + 2 + 1 = 7 games
Shortcut: 8 teams − 1 champion = 7 losers = 7 games
Common mistakes:
Counting teams instead of games (gets 8)

Tier 3 Total revenue?

ANSWER: $6,000
What students see:
Section A: $25 each, 120 tickets | Section B: $15 each, 200 tickets
Solution:
  • Section A: 25 × 120 = $3,000
  • Section B: 15 × 200 = $3,000
  • Total: $6,000

Varsity Each player's points?

ANSWER: Alex = 28, Jordan = 14, Casey = 10
What students see:
Three players scored 52 points total
• Alex: 2× Jordan's pts
• Jordan: Casey + 4 pts
Solution:
  1. Let C = Casey's points
  2. Jordan = C + 4
  3. Alex = 2(C + 4) = 2C + 8
  4. Total: C + (C + 4) + (2C + 8) = 52
    4C + 12 = 52
    4C = 40
    C = 10
  5. Casey = 10, Jordan = 14, Alex = 28
Check: 10 + 14 + 28 = 52 ✓
If student is stuck:
"Use a letter for Casey. Write Jordan and Alex using that letter. Add them up and set equal to 52."
Common mistakes:
Making Alex = 2 × Casey (should be 2 × Jordan)
Forgetting to distribute: 2(C+4) = 2C + 8, not 2C + 4

Verification Tips

When checking student answers:
  1. Ask them to explain — per the rules, they must explain or lose points
  2. Quick verbal checks:
    • "Walk me through your steps"
    • "How did you get that number?"
    • "What operation did you use?"
  3. For Varsity: Check their written work, not just final answer
